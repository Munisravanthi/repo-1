AGRICULATURE:

Agriculture, also known as farming is the practice of cultivating land and raising crops or livestock to produce food, fiber, and other goods for human use. It's a fundamental activity that supports human civilization, providing sustenance and economic gain. Agriculture encompasses various activities like planting, harvesting, and managing crops and animals, along with the preparation distribution of agricultural products.

Agriculture crop and livestock production, aquaculture, and forestry for food and non-food products. Agriculture was a key factor in the rise of sedentary human civilization, whereby farming of domesticated species created food surpluses that enabled people to live in the cities.

Types of Agriculture

  1. Subsistence Farming
  2. Commercial Farming
  3. Organic Farming
  4. dryland farming
  5. plantation farming

Subsistence farming

This type of farming is characterized by producing food and raising livestock primarily for the farmer's own consumption and the needs of their family.

Dryland farming

  • It's a sustainable agricultural practice
  • It's essential for food security in many countries, including India
  • It's especially beneficial for farmers with unreliable water sources
